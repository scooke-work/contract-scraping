# B2B Contract Negotiation — Key Findings by Persona

Distilled from the [`negotiation-playbook.md`](negotiation-playbook.md) and
[`vendor-defense-playbook.md`](vendor-defense-playbook.md). **Note the lens:** *Sales* and
*Legal* are the vendor's own functions; *Procurement* is the buyer across the table — so its
findings are the mirror image (where to push, not where to hold).

> *General market-practice reference, not legal advice — adapt with counsel.*

## 🟦 Sales (vendor) — close fast, protect margin, don't over-promise

*Your job: anchor confidently, concede predictably, never promise what Legal will reject.*

- **Most redlines have a known landing — don't panic.** Every clause has a market meeting point; an aggressive customer markup ≠ a deal at risk. Walk the fallback ladder and **trade every give for a get**.
- **Margin lives at renewal.** The highest-value commercial terms are the **escalation cap** (open 5–7%, land 3–5%) and keeping it **off your discounted rate**, not list. Defend the auto-renewal default — the customer's leverage collapses once switching costs sink.
- **Know your lane.** Self-serve within the ladder; escalate the instant an ask crosses a trigger: **any cap above 1×, anything uncapped (data/IP/liability), whole-contract refunds, MFN/parity, source-code escrow.** The Approval/Escalation Matrix is your cheat sheet.
- **Cheap gives that close deals:** a bare logo/reference, net-30→45, a mutual cap, a chronic-failure termination right (free if you perform). Spend these freely.
- **Protect the booking:** never agree to open-ended acceptance with no deemed-acceptance date — it delays revenue recognition (and your commission).
- **Read leverage:** deal size, a credible competitor, a multi-year commit, or a marquee-logo reference all change what you can hold.

## 🟩 Legal (vendor) — insurability, enforceability, precedent

*Your job: hold the lines tied to insurability and one-paper economics; concede bounded, insurable, self-controlled risk.*

- **The architecture:** hold the general **1× cap**, the **category-level consequential-damages waiver**, and **platform IP ownership**; concede **super-caps pegged to the cyber tower** (2–3× for data/IP) and the uncapped heads law won't let you cap anyway (gross negligence, willful misconduct, fraud, bodily injury).
- **The most-missed lever:** carve-outs must escape the consequential-damages **exclusion**, not just the dollar cap — a "capped-and-excluded" indemnity is **worthless**. Check indemnity, data breach, confidentiality.
- **Expect mutuality.** Cap, indemnity, assignment/CoC, jury waiver, non-solicit all go mutual — concede it cheaply (usually symmetric in your favor).
- **Doctrinal landmines:** UCC 2-719(2) FOEP (pair sole-remedy with a failure backstop **and** a cumulative-remedies carve-out); present-tense "hereby assigns" + work-for-hire backup (WFH alone fails for software); residuals vs. DTSA/UTSA; GDPR Art. 28/33 (direct subprocessor liability + breach clock — hold 48–72h, not 24h); §365 ipso facto unenforceability; the **fraud carve-out can't be drafted out** of the entire-agreement/non-reliance clause.
- **Data/privacy is the hot zone:** super-cap + consequential carve-out, breach-notice clock, subprocessor liability, and the **no-AI-training / no-secondary-use covenant** — the sharpest 2025–26 lever.
- **Employment currency:** California (+ ND/OK) void employee non-solicits (B&P 16600.5); the FTC non-compete rule was vacated — state law governs.
- **Stop Sales overwriting you:** precedence must keep the **MSA on top for legal terms**, with Order-Form overrides only by an express, signed, section-specific reference.

## 🟧 Procurement (buyer) — the mirror: where to push

*Your lens flips the vendor playbook. Spend leverage where the money and risk actually are; don't burn cycles on losing battles; lock the exit while you still can.*

- **Spend leverage here (high ROI):** escalation cap **off your discounted rate** (the list-price base silently claws back your year-1 discount); **data-breach super-cap + carve-outs**; IP indemnity; and **exit** — portable-format export + a **transition rate card locked at signing**.
- **Don't fight the unwinnable:** the AS-IS disclaimer, "no uninterrupted/error-free," the mutual consequential-damages waiver, mid-term convenience exit on a subscription, and the **99.9%** uptime number rarely move. Concede them; redirect to the **mechanics** (SLA credits, chronic-failure exit, carve-outs).
- **Pre-signature is peak leverage.** Win uplift caps, portability, transition rates, and price *before* switching costs sink — auto-renewal + uplift is exactly how the vendor recovers margin once you're locked in.
- **Demand mutuality + real carve-outs:** make the cap/indemnity/assignment mutual, and insist carve-outs escape the **consequential-damages exclusion** — or the indemnity you "won" is worthless.
- **Regulated/data-sensitive?** Your own GDPR/CCPA/sector duties set the non-negotiables (breach clock, subprocessor controls, audit/step-in, no-AI-training) — bring them on **your** addendum; they're compliance necessities, not asks.
- **Silent traps:** the **precedence clause** (a sales-drafted Order Form on top can overwrite negotiated terms — require MSA-controls + signed section overrides) and **operative notices** (need confirmed delivery, not email-to-account).
- **Your leverage drivers:** a documented credible alternative, deal size, and a multi-year/volume commitment (trade it for price protection and lower caps).
