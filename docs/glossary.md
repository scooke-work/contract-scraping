# Glossary & Acronym Key

Plain-English definitions for the terms and acronyms used across this knowledge base
(the [playbooks](vendor-defense-playbook.md), [clause bank](clause-library.md),
[risk matrix](clause-risk-matrix.md), [MSA checklist](msa-review-checklist.md), and
[benchmark](edgar-benchmark.md)). Definitions are contract-context, not exhaustive legal ones.

## Acronyms

| Acronym | Expands to | In one line |
|---|---|---|
| **ABAC** | Anti-Bribery & Anti-Corruption | Controls/representations against bribery and improper payments. |
| **ARR** | Annual Recurring Revenue | The recurring subscription revenue a vendor is valued on — why it defends auto-renewal/term. |
| **AUP** | Acceptable Use Policy | Rules for permitted use of a service. |
| **BAA** | Business Associate Agreement | HIPAA-required data agreement when handling protected health information. |
| **BCP / DR** | Business Continuity Plan / Disaster Recovery | The vendor's resilience obligations; often carved out of force majeure. |
| **CCPA** | California Consumer Privacy Act | California's consumer-privacy law. |
| **CGL** | Commercial General Liability | Baseline business-liability insurance. |
| **CIK** | Central Index Key | A filer's unique ID in SEC EDGAR. |
| **CISG** | UN Convention on Contracts for the International Sale of Goods | Default law for cross-border B2B goods sales. |
| **CoC** | Change of Control | A party being acquired / majority ownership changing. |
| **COI** | Certificate of Insurance | Proof a policy exists (weaker than an actual endorsement). |
| **CPI** | Consumer Price Index | Inflation index sometimes used to set renewal price increases. |
| **DORA** | Digital Operational Resilience Act | EU financial-sector resilience/third-party regime. |
| **DPA** | Data Processing Agreement / Addendum | The data-protection terms, usually a separate exhibit. |
| **DPF** | (EU-US) Data Privacy Framework | A lawful cross-border data-transfer mechanism. |
| **DTSA / UTSA** | Defend Trade Secrets Act / Uniform Trade Secrets Act | US trade-secret law (why trade-secret confidentiality is indefinite). |
| **E&O** | Errors & Omissions | Professional-liability insurance (a.k.a. Tech E&O for software). |
| **EDGAR** | Electronic Data Gathering, Analysis, and Retrieval | The SEC's public filings system this scraper queries. |
| **EULA** | End User License Agreement | License terms for software use. |
| **EX-10** | Exhibit 10 ("Material Contracts") | The SEC exhibit type that carries real B2B agreements. |
| **FCPA** | Foreign Corrupt Practices Act | US anti-bribery statute. |
| **FFIEC / NYDFS** | Federal Financial Institutions Examination Council / NY Dept. of Financial Services | US financial regulators with third-party/security rules. |
| **FOEP** | Failure of Essential Purpose | UCC 2-719(2): a limited remedy that fails its purpose may be unenforceable. |
| **GDPR** | General Data Protection Regulation | EU data-protection law (e.g., 72-hour breach notice). |
| **GLBA** | Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act | US financial-data privacy law. |
| **IDTA** | International Data Transfer Agreement | UK cross-border data-transfer mechanism. |
| **IP** | Intellectual Property | Patents, copyrights, trademarks, trade secrets. |
| **ISO 27001** | (information-security standard) | Certifiable infosec management standard. |
| **LD** | Liquidated Damages | A pre-agreed damages amount (e.g., for non-solicit breach). |
| **LoL** | Limitation of Liability | The clause that caps and excludes damages. |
| **MFN** | Most-Favored-Customer / -Nation | A promise of pricing at least as good as comparable customers. |
| **MSA** | Master Services Agreement | The master legal framework; SOWs/Order Forms sit under it. |
| **NDA** | Non-Disclosure Agreement | Standalone confidentiality agreement. |
| **OEM** | Original Equipment Manufacturer | A reseller/embedding arrangement. |
| **SCC** | Standard Contractual Clauses | EU-approved cross-border data-transfer terms. |
| **SLA** | Service Level Agreement | Uptime/performance commitments + service credits. |
| **SOC 2** | System and Organization Controls 2 | A widely required security audit report (Type II = over a period). |
| **SOW** | Statement of Work | The document defining a specific engagement's scope/price/deliverables. |
| **UAT** | User Acceptance Testing | The buyer's testing of a deliverable before acceptance. |
| **UCC** | Uniform Commercial Code | US state law governing sale of goods (Article 2). |
| **UKBA** | UK Bribery Act | UK anti-bribery statute (broader than the FCPA). |

## Key terms

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Additional insured** | Naming the other party on your insurance so it's covered under your policy. |
| **Anti-stacking** | Language ensuring multiple claims share one cap rather than each getting its own. |
| **Background / pre-existing IP** | IP a party owned before the deal; each side keeps its own. |
| **Benchmarking** | Comparing the deal's pricing to the market (often via an independent benchmarker). |
| **Cap** | The ceiling on a party's total liability (often 1× trailing-12-month fees). |
| **Carve-out** | An exception that escapes a cap or waiver (e.g., IP, confidentiality, fraud). |
| **Change order** | A written change to a SOW's scope/price. |
| **Choice of law / governing law** | Which jurisdiction's law interprets the contract. |
| **Consequential damages** | Indirect/downstream losses (lost profits, lost data) — usually mutually waived. |
| **Controller / processor / subprocessor** | GDPR roles: who decides the data use vs. who processes it vs. a processor's vendor. |
| **Cumulative remedies** | Remedies add up (vs. "sole and exclusive") — must be reconciled with sole-remedy clauses. |
| **Deemed acceptance** | A deliverable counts as accepted after silence or production use. |
| **Evergreen / auto-renewal** | The contract renews automatically unless notice is given. |
| **Escalation cap / uplift** | The limit on how much fees can rise at renewal. |
| **Force majeure** | Excuse for non-performance due to events beyond a party's control. |
| **Holdback** | Withholding part of a fee until acceptance. |
| **Indemnify / defend** | Pay for (and run the defense of) specified third-party claims. |
| **Key personnel** | Named individuals a buyer relies on; often locked against reassignment. |
| **Mutuality** | A term applying symmetrically to both parties (the goal of sophisticated redlines). |
| **Net 30 / 45 / 60** | Payment due that many days after invoice. |
| **Non-reliance** | A clause stating neither party relied on statements outside the contract (parol-evidence shield). |
| **Notice-and-prejudice** | Late claim notice only reduces the indemnity to the extent it caused harm. |
| **Novation** | Substituting a new party (transferring both benefits *and* obligations). |
| **Order of precedence** | The rule for which document wins when MSA/Order Form/exhibits conflict. |
| **Perfect tender** | UCC right (goods) to reject anything that doesn't exactly conform. |
| **Residuals** | A right to use general knowledge retained in unaided memory (erodes confidentiality). |
| **Set-off** | Netting an amount you're owed against an amount you owe. |
| **Sole & exclusive remedy** | The only remedy available for a given breach (e.g., SLA credits). |
| **Supercap** | A higher, separate liability cap for high-severity heads (e.g., data breach). |
| **Title vs. risk of loss** | (Goods) who *owns* vs. who *bears loss of* the goods — can pass at different points. |
| **Transition assistance** | The vendor's help (at a rate card) moving the buyer off the service at exit. |
| **Walkaway** | The position past which a party won't sign — it escalates to the top or kills the deal. |
| **Work-made-for-hire** | Ownership of created work vesting in the buyer (paired with a present assignment, since WFH alone fails for software). |
